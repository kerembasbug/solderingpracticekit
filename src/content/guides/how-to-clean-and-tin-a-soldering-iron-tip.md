A clean, well-tinned tip is the difference between soldering that feels effortless and soldering that feels impossible. Tips are also the part that wears out, so a few good habits save you money. Here's everything you need to know.

## What "tinning" means and why it matters

**Tinning** is keeping a thin layer of solder on the tip. That layer:

- Transfers heat efficiently from the tip into the joint
- Protects the tip from oxidizing (going black and dead)
- Lets solder flow where you want it

A shiny, silver tip works. A dull, blackened tip does not — no matter how hot it is — because the oxide layer blocks heat transfer.

## Cleaning a tip during use

While soldering, clean the tip between joints:

1. Wipe it on **brass wool** (preferred) or a damp sponge.
2. Re-tin with a touch of fresh solder if it looks dry.

**Brass wool beats a wet sponge:** a wet sponge shock-cools the tip each wipe, which stresses the plating and cools your work. Brass wool cleans without the temperature drop.

## Reviving a black, oxidized tip

If your tip has already gone dark and won't take solder:

1. Make sure the iron is at full working temperature.
2. Wipe firmly on brass wool.
3. Apply fresh solder with plenty of flux, or use a dedicated **tip tinner / activator** paste — push the tip into it and the mild abrasive plus flux strips the oxide.
4. Wipe and re-tin. Repeat until the working face is bright silver.

Avoid filing or sanding plated tips — that removes the iron plating and ruins them permanently. Only bare copper tips can be (gently) reshaped.

## Habits that make tips last

- **Set the right temperature.** Running needlessly hot (above ~370°C / 700°F) accelerates oxidation. Use the lowest temp that makes a clean joint.
- **Keep it tinned.** Never leave a bare tip sitting hot.
- **Use the auto-sleep** feature if your iron has one (the [YIHUA 926 III](/reviews/yihua-926-iii-soldering-station) does) — it drops the temperature when idle.
- **Tin before you switch off.** Leave a blob of solder on the tip; clean it next time you start.
- **Ventilate and use rosin-core solder** so flux keeps the tip active.

Look after your tips and a single one can last for years. Pair good tip care with the right [solder](/guides/lead-free-vs-leaded-solder) and a [practice kit](/best-soldering-practice-kits), and your joints will improve immediately.
